Daily Life

Gan Katan is built for the way young children actually grow.

Children arrive into warm routines, language-rich classrooms, and teachers who know how to help them settle, participate, and feel proud of themselves.

They sing, move, explore, build friendships, and learn how to care for their belongings, use their words, and join the shared rhythm of the day.

That is why families choose Gan Katan. It feels joyful. It feels personal. And it helps children grow into confident little people.

Warm routines

Children know what to expect, so they feel safe enough to explore.

Independence by design

Teachers coach language, self-help skills, and pride in doing things well.

Joyful belonging

Children are welcomed into a classroom culture that feels close, calm, and alive.

Why Gan Katan

Why parents choose Gan Katan.

They want more than childcare. They want a preschool where children are known well, Jewish life feels natural, and family life feels calmer.

Children are known well.

Close teaching starts early with 11 to 20 students and 2+ teachers in every classroom, so children are seen, supported, and gently guided throughout the day.

Jewish life feels natural.

Songs, blessings, celebrations, and everyday mitzvot are part of the rhythm of school, so Jewish identity feels lived, warm, and joyful from the start.

Families feel the difference at home.

Children leave with more confidence, stronger language, and a greater sense of belonging. Parents feel that growth in the way their children talk, connect, and carry themselves.

Practical Details

The details parents want to know.

Gan Katan serves children from 18 months to 5 years, with each class following an age-right rhythm and a warm, structured day.

Ages

18 months to 5 years

Children grow through four classroom stages, each designed for the next step in independence, language, and belonging.

Classrooms

Daisies to Butterflies

Daisies, Sunflowers, Caterpillars, and Butterflies. Each classroom is grouped by age and has its own daily rhythm.

Teaching

11 to 20 students. 2+ teachers.

Children get real attention, not one adult managing a crowd. Teachers can coach language, routines, social growth, and early learning in real time.

The Day

Joyful, structured, and active

Songs, movement, hands-on exploration, and Jewish rhythms shape the day. Each class has its own schedule, with highlights tailored by age.
