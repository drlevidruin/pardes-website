The Pardes Way

Education redesigned.
On purpose.

We rebuilt how school works so children get more attention, more practice, and more purpose every single day.

Multiple Teachers.
Precision Academics.

In every classroom, multiple teachers work with students simultaneously across Judaic Studies, General Studies, and independent work. Students are known well, challenged well, and taught in small groups every single day.

Teachers use constant observation and NWEA MAP Growth data to keep instruction moving. Children are not left waiting for the room, and they are not asked to disappear into the background.

Every child gets direct instruction, every day.

Practical Jewish Living.

Judaism at Pardes is something students experience, appreciate, and make their own. From Chumash and Kriah to Shabbat assembly and a deep love for Israel.

Jewish identity is woven through every day, not confined to a single class period. Students do not just study their heritage. They live it.

Judaism is not a subject here. It is how we live.

Built for Life,
Not Just School.

Collaboration, emotional intelligence, handling disappointment, forgiveness. At Pardes, real-life skills are taught alongside Chumash and math, because both matter equally.

And practice time is built into the school day with teachers guiding students through problem-solving. Less homework stress at the kitchen table. More room for family life in the evening.

Families get their evenings back.

Common Questions

What parents ask us.

What makes The Pardes Way different from other schools?

Every classroom is built for close teaching. Multiple teachers are working simultaneously, students get direct instruction every day, Jewish life is woven into the rhythm of school, and practice happens during the day instead of turning evenings into homework battles. The result is a school that feels more personal, more purposeful, and calmer for families.

How is Jewish life woven into the day?

Jewish life is not tucked into one corner of the schedule. Students learn Chumash and Kriah, sing at Shabbat assembly, speak about middot, and grow up inside a school culture where Judaism is lived, shared, and loved.

Why do families say life feels calmer at Pardes?

Because the day is built differently. Students get guided practice in school, teachers know them well, and families are not spending the evening in nightly homework stress. Home can feel like home again.
