Our Story

From five children and a living room to over 220 students and two campuses. We're just getting started.

Where It Began

It started with five children and a living room.

In 2011, Katy Horowitz moved from London to Miami Beach with a simple belief: Jewish education could be joyful, rigorous, and built for how children actually learn. She started with five students in a home daycare. That grew into Preschool (Gan Katan), then a kindergarten in 2017, then a grade added every year.

In 2024, the school purchased its building at 1211 Marseille Dr via an SBA loan after the community raised over $400,000, a testament to the families who believed in what Pardes was building.

Today, Pardes Day School serves over 220 students across two campuses from preschool through 8th grade. The building changed. The mission never did.

Our Mission

Why we exist.

"To provide a joyful, rigorous education that develops the whole child academically, socially, and as a proud member of the Jewish community."

  • Right level, right pace. Every child learns at exactly the right level, not waiting and not struggling to keep pace
  • Multiple teachers in every classroom so no child is ever just a face in the crowd
  • Practice time built into the school day with trained teacher support
  • Jewish identity woven through every day, experienced, appreciated, and made personal
  • Real-life skills taught alongside academics: collaboration, emotional intelligence, resilience
  • A community where families feel at home before day one

History

How we got here.

  1. 2011

    The Beginning

    Katy Horowitz starts a home daycare with five children in Miami Beach.

  2. 2013

    Preschool (Gan Katan) Opens

    Preschool (Gan Katan) officially opens, bringing The Pardes Way to more Miami Beach families.

  3. 2017

    Elementary Begins

    Kindergarten launches. The elementary school takes its first steps.

  4. 2018–2022

    Grade by Grade

    One grade added every year through 5th grade, building a complete K-5 elementary program.

  5. 2023

    Middle School Opens

    Middle School at Pardes (MSAP) opens at 7055 Bonita Dr, extending The Pardes Way through 8th grade.

  6. 2024

    A Building of Our Own

    The school purchases its main campus building at 1211 Marseille Dr, supported by an SBA loan and over $400,000 raised by the community.

Accreditation

Pardes Day School's elementary program is accredited by the Association of Independent Schools of Florida (AISF). We are a Miami Jewish Federation funded school.

Non-Discrimination Policy

We embrace and value differences, such as ethnicity and national origin, religious denomination and spiritual practice, race, age, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, socio-economic levels and mental and physical ability.
