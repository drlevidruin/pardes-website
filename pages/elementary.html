Academics

Skills and content, working together.

Our English curriculum, designed by our Head of School, ensures students leave with a love for reading, the skills to write, and the ability to engage in conversation, all on a personal level.

Math instruction uses HMH Into Math, and we track growth with NWEA MAP Growth assessments.

Afternoons bring those skills to life through science, social studies, and real-world projects that make learning meaningful.

Multiple teachers working simultaneously in every classroom means we respond to each learner's pace and needs by design.

Judaics

Practical, joyful Jewish education.

Hebrew language, Jewish texts, and living values. Rooted in Chabad tradition, connected to real life.

Judaics

Practical, joyful Jewish education.

We use Torah 4 Children for Kriah, Bright Beginnings and Lashon Hatorah for Chumash, and Bright Beginnings for Gemara.

All instruction is guided by the JSAT standards from COJDS to ensure students build real skills, not just test scores.

Students see how Torah wisdom, mitzvot, and tradition guide daily decisions, build relationships, and strengthen community.

Real-Life Skills

Character woven into everything.

Real-life skills are not a separate program at Pardes. They are embedded in how we teach, how we greet each other, and how we resolve challenges.

LeHashlim, our real-life skills curriculum, teaches collaboration, emotional management, and the skills the real world actually demands.

Our teachers are skilled coaches. They notice when a student is struggling, model respectful communication, and help children build confidence and empathy.

Enrichment & Future Ready

Building skills for the real world.

We introduce technology purposefully, ensuring it enhances learning rather than replaces thinking.

Starting in 3rd grade, students explore foundational technology, including age-appropriate AI literacy and Waggle mathematics.

Pardes graduates enter middle school with the skills and confidence to lead.

Homework

Practice where it counts. Everything else stays at school.

Families get their evenings back.

At Pardes, homework means reading review in both Hebrew and English. Practice, practice, practice. That is it.

All other learning happens during the school day with the support of our teaching teams. If a student does not finish their work during school hours, they may take it home over the weekend, but that is the exception, not the rule.

We believe family time matters. Evenings should be for dinner, conversation, and rest, not for worksheets.
