Why Families Choose Preschool (Gan Katan)

The Pardes Preschool difference.

An intentional, joyful environment where each child thrives.

  • Led by Sharone Eichberg, Director and Co-founder of Pardes Day School, who has been shaping the Gan Katan experience since the beginning.
  • Fluid instructional groups with multiple teachers working simultaneously in every classroom for intentional, responsive attention.
  • Routines by design. Carefully planned rhythms build independence, confidence, and emotional resilience from the start.
  • Practical Jewish living woven throughout. Songs, blessings, celebrations, and daily mitzvot make Jewish identity joyful and real.
  • Real-life skills embedded daily. We coach children consistently so they develop emotional awareness, confidence, and a sense of belonging.
  • Play as the foundation. Movement, exploration, and hands-on discovery strengthen learning and joy.
By the Numbers

Small classes. Big attention.

Every child is known, supported, and celebrated.

  • 11 to 20 students per class from our youngest learners to our oldest, so every child gets the attention they deserve.
  • 3 to 4 teachers in every classroom. Not one adult managing a crowd. A team of educators working together, every day.
  • Four classrooms by age: Daisies (turning 2), Sunflowers (turning 3), Caterpillars (turning 4), and Butterflies (turning 5 / PreK).
  • Each class has its own rhythm. Schedules are designed around what each age group needs, not a one-size-fits-all template.
Our Culture

Building independence, joy, and Jewish roots.

In Preschool (Gan Katan), every child learns to belong.

In Preschool (Gan Katan), children flourish in an environment built by design with warmth and intention. We help each child develop independence: caring for their belongings, using language to express needs, and participating proudly in shared routines.

Our teachers provide the structure and genuine connection that make children feel confident and cared for.

Through songs, blessings, celebrations, and everyday mitzvot, Jewish life is lived and practical, not distant or abstract. Children discover that being Jewish means caring for others, marking time with joy, and building community where everyone belongs.

By the time your child leaves Preschool (Gan Katan), they carry confidence, kindness, and their own connection to something meaningful.
