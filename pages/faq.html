Common Questions

Your questions, answered.

Pardes serves students from 18 months in Preschool (Gan Katan) through 8th Grade. Our three divisions, Preschool, Elementary K-5, and Middle School 6-8, each have their own leadership team while sharing one unified educational philosophy: The Pardes Way.

We redesigned how school works. Every classroom has multiple teachers working simultaneously with fluid instructional groups, so every child receives direct instruction at exactly the right level every day. Practice time is built into the school day with trained teacher support. Families get their evenings back. And we teach real-life skills alongside academics because both matter equally.

Fluid instructional grouping means students are regularly reassessed and placed in small groups based on their current mastery level, not a fixed track. Groups shift as students grow. This is powered by ongoing data from assessments like NWEA MAP Growth and JSAT standards, so every child is always working at exactly the right level of challenge.

Practice time is built into the school day at Pardes. Students work through problems and apply what they've learned with trained teachers guiding them in real time. This means less homework at home and more meaningful family time in the evenings.

Judaism at Pardes is something students experience, appreciate, and make their own. It is not just content to memorize. From Chumash and Kriah to Shabbat assembly, Israel education, and real mitzvah projects, Jewish identity is woven through every day. Our goal is for every student to leave Pardes as a proud, knowledgeable, and engaged member of the Jewish community.

Yes. Hebrew language is a core part of the Pardes curriculum across all divisions. Students develop reading, writing, and conversational Hebrew skills alongside their Judaic Studies program. By the time students complete elementary school, they have a strong functional foundation in the Hebrew language.

The Pardes model is built for differentiation. With multiple teachers in every classroom and fluid instructional groups, students who learn differently receive the direct, targeted support they need every single day. This is not an add-on. It is the normal rhythm of school. Our team regularly reviews student data to ensure every child is supported and appropriately challenged.
