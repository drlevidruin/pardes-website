Common Questions

What families ask us.

What grades does Pardes Day School serve?

Pardes serves students from 18 months in Preschool (Gan Katan) through 8th Grade. Our three divisions, Preschool, Elementary K-5, and Middle School 6-8, each have their own leadership team while sharing one unified educational philosophy: The Pardes Way.

What makes The Pardes Way different from other schools?

We redesigned how school works. Every classroom has multiple teachers working simultaneously with fluid instructional groups, so every child receives direct instruction at exactly the right level every day. Practice time is built into the school day with trained teacher support. Families get their evenings back. And we teach real-life skills alongside academics because both matter equally.

How does the admissions process work?

It starts with a campus tour where you can walk through real classrooms and meet our educators. From there, you submit an online application, meet with our admissions team, and, once accepted, receive clear onboarding with a buddy system so your family feels at home before day one. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis, with early submissions receiving priority.

Do you offer financial aid or scholarships?

Yes. Pardes offers need-based financial aid and merit scholarships. We believe every family that wants The Pardes Way for their child should have a path to get here. Contact our admissions team at (305) 690-2548 to discuss what options are available for your family.

What is "fluid instructional grouping"?

Fluid instructional grouping means students are regularly reassessed and placed in small groups based on their current mastery level, not a fixed track. Groups shift as students grow. This is powered by ongoing data from assessments like NWEA MAP Growth and JSAT standards, so every child is always working at exactly the right level of challenge.

Do students have homework?

Practice time is built into the school day at Pardes. Students work through problems and apply what they have learned with trained teachers guiding them in real time. This means less homework at home and more meaningful family time in the evenings.

How does Pardes approach Jewish education?

Judaism at Pardes is something students experience, appreciate, and make their own. It is not just content to memorize. From Chumash and Kriah to Shabbat assembly, Israel education, and real mitzvah projects, Jewish identity is woven through every day.

Is Hebrew language instruction included?

Yes. Hebrew language is a core part of the Pardes curriculum across all divisions. Students develop reading, writing, and conversational Hebrew skills alongside their Judaic Studies program.

How does Pardes support students who learn differently?

The Pardes model is built for differentiation. With multiple teachers in every classroom and fluid instructional groups, students who learn differently receive the direct, targeted support they need every single day. This is not an add-on. It is the normal rhythm of school.

What extracurricular activities are available?

Pardes offers a range of extracurricular programs including sports, arts, and enrichment activities. Our LeHashlim program provides additional academic and personal development opportunities. Contact our office for the current list of available programs.

Where is Pardes Day School located?

Pardes Day School operates across two campuses in Miami Beach. Our main campus is at 1211 Marseille Dr, Miami Beach, FL 33141 (Preschool and Elementary). Our Middle School at Pardes (MSAP) is at 7055 Bonita Dr, Miami Beach, FL 33141.
