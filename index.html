The Pardes Way

Education redesigned.
On purpose.

We rebuilt how school works so every child gets more instruction, more feedback, and a truly individualized experience.

Multiple Teachers.
Precision Academics.

This isn't an aide sitting in the corner. Multiple teachers work with students simultaneously across Judaic Studies, General Studies, and independent work. Every child receives direct, small-group instruction every single day.

Fluid instructional groups are constantly reassessed using NWEA MAP Growth data. Your child works at exactly the right level. No waiting for the class to catch up, no struggling to keep pace.

Every child gets direct instruction, every day.

Practical Jewish Living.

Judaism at Pardes is something students experience, appreciate, and make their own. From Chumash and Kriah to Shabbat assembly and a deep love for Israel.

Jewish identity is woven through every day, not confined to a single class period. Students don't just study their heritage. They live it.

Judaism isn't a subject here. It's how we live.

Built for Life,
Not Just School.

Collaboration, emotional intelligence, handling disappointment, forgiveness. Through our LeHashlim program, real-life skills are taught alongside Chumash and math, because both matter equally.

And practice time is built into the school day with trained teachers guiding students through problem-solving. No homework battles at the kitchen table. Families get their evenings back.

Families get their evenings back.

Common Questions

What parents ask us.

What makes The Pardes Way different from other schools?

Every classroom has multiple teachers working simultaneously, with classes of 11 to 20 students. Students receive direct instruction at exactly the right level every day through fluid instructional groups. Homework is reading review only, in both Hebrew and English. Everything else is done during the school day, so families get their evenings back. And through LeHashlim, our real-life skills curriculum, we teach collaboration, emotional management, and the skills the real world actually demands.

Do you offer financial aid or scholarships?

Yes. Pardes accepts all major Florida scholarship programs including Step Up For Students, FES-UA, FES-EO, and FTC. We also offer need-based support through our Ahavas Yeladim Fund. We believe every family that wants The Pardes Way for their child should have a path to get here.

How does the admissions process work?

Start by filling out an inquiry form. Our office will reach out to schedule a tour at a time that works for your family. After your tour, we schedule a parent conversation. We interview parents, not children, because the strongest partnerships start with understanding how families and school can grow together. Admissions are rolling with no fixed deadlines.
