The Pardes Way

Education redesigned. On purpose.

We rebuilt how school works so every child gets more instruction, more feedback, and a truly individualized experience.

Multiple Teachers. Every Classroom. Every Day.

This isn't an aide sitting in the corner. Multiple teachers work with students simultaneously across Judaic Studies, General Studies, and independent work. Every child receives direct, small-group instruction every single day.

Precision Academics

Fluid instructional groups are constantly reassessed using NWEA MAP Growth and JSAT standards. Your child works at exactly the right level — not waiting for the class to catch up, not struggling to keep pace.

Practical Jewish Living

Judaism at Pardes is something students experience, appreciate, and make their own. From Chumash and Kriah to Shabbat assembly and a love for Israel — Jewish identity woven through every day.

Practice Where It Counts

Practice time is built into the school day where trained teachers guide students through problem-solving. No more homework battles. Families get their evenings back.

Built for Life, Not Just School

Collaboration, emotional intelligence, handling disappointment, forgiveness. Real-life skills are taught alongside Chumash and math — because both matter equally.

Frequently asked questions.

Pardes serves students from 18 months (Preschool / Gan Katan) through 8th Grade. Our three divisions — Preschool, Elementary (K-5), and Middle School (6-8) — each have their own leadership team while sharing one unified educational philosophy: The Pardes Way.

We redesigned how school works. Every classroom has multiple teachers working simultaneously with fluid instructional groups, so every child receives direct instruction at exactly the right level every day. Practice time is built into the school day with trained teacher support — families get their evenings back. And we teach real-life skills alongside academics because both matter equally.

It starts with a campus tour where you can walk through real classrooms and meet our educators. From there, you submit an online application, meet with our admissions team, and — once accepted — receive clear onboarding with a buddy system so your family feels at home before day one. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis, with early submissions receiving priority.

Practice time is built into the school day at Pardes. Students work through problems and apply what they've learned with trained teachers guiding them in real time. This means less homework at home and more meaningful family time in the evenings.

Our Story

It started with five children and a living room.

In 2011, Katy Horowitz moved from London to Miami Beach with a simple belief: Jewish education could be joyful, rigorous, and built for how children actually learn. She started with five students in a home daycare. That grew into Gan Katan preschool, then a kindergarten in 2017, then a grade added every year.

Today, Pardes Day School serves over 220 students across two campuses from preschool through 8th grade — and we're still growing. The building changed. The mission never did.
