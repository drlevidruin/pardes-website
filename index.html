The Pardes Way

Education redesigned.
On purpose.

We rebuilt how school works so every child gets more instruction, more feedback, and a truly individualized experience.

Multiple Teachers.
Precision Academics.

This isn't an aide sitting in the corner. Multiple teachers work with students simultaneously across Judaic Studies, General Studies, and independent work. Every child receives direct, small-group instruction every single day.

Fluid instructional groups are constantly reassessed using NWEA MAP Growth data. Your child works at exactly the right level. No waiting for the class to catch up, no struggling to keep pace.

Every child gets direct instruction, every day.

Practical Jewish Living.

Judaism at Pardes is something students experience, appreciate, and make their own. From Chumash and Kriah to Shabbat assembly and a deep love for Israel.

Jewish identity is woven through every day, not confined to a single class period. Students don't just study their heritage. They live it.

Judaism isn't a subject here. It's how we live.

Built for Life,
Not Just School.

Collaboration, emotional intelligence, handling disappointment, forgiveness. Through our LeHashlim program, real-life skills are taught alongside Chumash and math, because both matter equally.

And practice time is built into the school day with trained teachers guiding students through problem-solving. No homework battles at the kitchen table. Families get their evenings back.

Families get their evenings back.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions.

Pardes serves students from 18 months in Preschool (Gan Katan) through 8th Grade. Our three divisions, Preschool, Elementary K-5, and Middle School 6-8, each have their own leadership team while sharing one unified educational philosophy: The Pardes Way.

Every classroom has multiple teachers working simultaneously, with classes of 11 to 20 students and 3 to 4 teachers per room. Students receive direct instruction at exactly the right level every day through fluid instructional groups. Homework is reading review only, in both Hebrew and English. Everything else is done during the school day, so families get their evenings back. And through LeHashlim, our real-life skills curriculum, we teach collaboration, emotional management, and the skills the real world actually demands.

Our Story

It started with five children and a living room.

In 2011, Katy Horowitz moved from London to Miami Beach with a simple belief: Jewish education could be joyful, rigorous, and built for how children actually learn. She started with five students in a home daycare. That grew into Preschool (Gan Katan), then a kindergarten in 2017, then a grade added every year.

Today, Pardes Day School serves over 220 students across two campuses from preschool through 8th grade. And we are still growing. The building changed. The mission never did.
